About me

I am a professor of Political Science at High Point University in North Carolina, where I teach comparative politics, international relations, and various specialized seminars. From 2007 to 2017, I served as the founding chair of the university's Department of Political Science and have directed the university's International Relations major program since 2009.

I grew up on a mule ranch in Idaho and received my BA with majors in political science, international affairs, and Spanish literature from Pacific University in Oregon. I worked briefly on Capitol Hil before completing a PhD in Government is from the University of Texas at Austin. After holding visiting appointments at the University of Portland and Lewis & Clark College, I joined HPU's faculty in 2004.

More information on my background, training, and scholarship can be found in my curriculum vitae.
